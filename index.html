Blank Tape

Old n-gram models guess the next word from raw frequency. They don't know who "she" refers to, or that a shy twin is about to surprise everyone. Feed them a story, punch a hole where the word should go, and watch a thousand guesses come out of the machine — then see how a real language model handles the same hole.

02 Pick models to compare

The LLM option downloads a ~65 MB model the first time (cached after that) and runs it fully in your browser — no server, no API key. It fills the blank using both left and right context, which is exactly what the n-gram models can't do.

03 Why this happens

A bigram model only ever looks at the single word before the blank ("was") and asks: across everything it's seen, what word most often follows "was"? A trigram looks back two words. Neither has any idea that the sentence is about which sister spoke, because that information lives many words earlier and depends on understanding the story, not just counting word pairs. A modern language model attends to the whole passage — including the setup about Mia being outgoing and Maya being shy — and can use the twist ("but when...") to guess the less obvious name. That gap is the whole demo.
