  <section class="panel" id="input-panel">

    <h2>Set up the passage</h2>

    <label class="field-label" for="prompt">Prompt <span class="hint">— write the story, then mark each word to guess with <code>&lt;blank&gt;</code>. You can use more than one <code>&lt;blank&gt;</code> in the same passage.</span></label>

    <textarea id="prompt" rows="9">Maya and Mia had been inseparable since kindergarten, but everyone in town knew they were opposites. Mia was the one who volunteered for every talent show and student council race, while Maya spent most assemblies hiding behind her sister, mortified at the thought of a microphone. So when the graduation committee announced that only one twin would deliver the commencement speech, the whole gym assumed it would be the outgoing one. But when the lights came up and a name was called, it was <blank> who walked, trembling, to the podium.</textarea>


    <div class="field-group wide">

    <p class="hint tiny">Every model below — <b>n-grams (unigram/bigram/trigram/4-gram), embeddings, and

    RNN</b> — trains its background signal on a bundled real-text sample (~1GB of Wikipedia text, split

    into chunk files in <code>static-corpus/</code> and shipped with this site — no external API call, no

    live network dependency). Your story is trained alongside it and repeated so a well-attested

    story-specific pattern can still win out over the generic background.</p>

    </div>


    <div class="field-group">

      <label class="field-label" for="runs">Samples per model</label>

      <input type="number" id="runs" value="1000" min="10" max="5000" step="10">

    </div>


    <h2>Pick models to compare</h2>

    <div class="model-picker" id="model-picker">

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="unigram" checked><span>Unigram</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="Guesses from raw word frequency — no context. Blends your story's own word counts with frequency from the bundled real-text background corpus.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="bigram" checked><span>Bigram</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="Looks at the single word right before the blank. Blends whatever your story itself has shown following that word with the same 1-word context in the bundled background corpus.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="trigram" checked><span>Trigram</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="Looks at the two words before the blank. Blends your story's own evidence with that exact 2-word context in the bundled background corpus — sharper than bigram when that pair is real, common English.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="fourgram"><span>4-gram</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="Looks at the three words before the blank. Blends your story's own evidence with that exact 3-word context in the bundled background corpus; backs off toward shorter contexts (Kneser-Ney) if that exact run of words has never occurred.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="embeddings" checked><span>Embeddings (PPMI + SVD)</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="Turns each word into a vector based on which words tend to sit near it, then averages the vectors on both sides of the blank and finds the closest word to that average.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="rnn"><span>RNN (live-trained)</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="A tiny recurrent network trained on the fly. It reads left-to-right, updating one running 'memory' vector word by word, instead of just counting fixed windows like the n-grams.">i</span>

      </label>

      <label class="chip llm-chip">

        <input type="checkbox" value="llm" id="llm-toggle"><span>Small LLM (in-browser, DistilBERT)</span>

        <span class="info-dot" tabindex="0" data-tip="A real pretrained transformer. It reads the whole passage — both before and after the blank — at once using attention, instead of hand-built statistics.">i</span>

      </label>

    </div>

    <p class="hint tiny">Six stages of the same field, each on the same blank: <b>unigram → bigram → trigram/4-gram</b> (frequency tables,

    1980s–2000s, trained here on your story blended with a bundled real-text background corpus) → <b>embeddings</b> (words as

    vectors from co-occurrence statistics, the word2vec/GloVe-era idea, 2003–2013) →

    <b>RNN</b> (a running hidden state instead of a fixed window, Mikolov 2010) → <b>LLM</b> (a real pretrained transformer using

    full bidirectional context, 2018–present).</p>

    <p class="hint tiny" id="llm-note">The LLM option downloads a ~65&nbsp;MB model the first time (cached after that) and runs it fully in your browser — no server, no API key.</p>
